Honoring Dr. José Rizal
"He who does not love his own language is worse than a beast and a stinking fish." – José Rizal

Rizal Day - December 30

Rizal Day is celebrated every December 30 to honor the life and contributions of our national hero, Dr. José Rizal.

Rizal was a man of many talents—a writer, doctor, linguist, and nationalist. His works "Noli Me Tangere" and "El Filibusterismo" inspired the revolution against Spanish rule, igniting a movement for Filipino independence.

Beyond his death, his ideals continue to inspire Filipinos to value education, patriotism, and integrity.

Rizal’s Achievements

  • Fluent in more than 20 languages
  • Wrote novels that exposed Spanish abuses
  • Established La Liga Filipina
  • Worked as an ophthalmologist to help the blind
  • Fought for peaceful reform in the Philippines
